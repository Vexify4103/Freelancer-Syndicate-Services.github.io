## The Mining Loop

Mining in Star Citizen is one of the most rewarding gameplay loops — but it takes some practice. Here's how it works:

1. **Travel** to a mining site (asteroid belt or planet surface)
2. **Scan** for ore deposits
3. **Fracture** large rocks into mineable chunks
4. **Extract** ore into your cargo hold
5. **Sell** at a commodity terminal or refinery

## Best Ships for Mining

| Ship | Type | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Prospector | Solo miner | Best solo option, affordable |
| MOLE | Multi-crew | 3-person, high yield |
| ROC | Ground vehicle | Surface mining only |

## Finding Good Rocks

Use your **Mining Mode** (`M`) to toggle scanning. Look for rocks with:

- **High percentage** of valuable minerals (Quantanium, Bexalite, Laranite)
- **Resistance** in the right range for your laser
- **Low instability** to avoid blow-ups

### Valuable Ore Tiers

| Tier | Minerals | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| High value | Quantanium, Bexalite | High risk/reward, very unstable |
| Mid value | Laranite, Borase | Safe, consistent income |
| Low value | Titanium, Corundum | Good for padding cargo |

## Fracturing Safely

When you fire your mining laser, watch the **charge bar**:

- Keep it in the **green zone** for steady fracturing
- Entering the **red zone** risks an explosion — back off immediately
- Use **Fracturing Modules** on your ship to widen the safe window

## Selling Your Ore

Fly to a **Commodity Terminal** at any major station or city. Look for high buy prices using trade tools like [sc-trade.tools](https://sc-trade.tools).

Alternatively, drop ore at a **Refinery** (e.g., Grim HEX, CRU-L1) to refine it into a higher-value processed form — takes time but pays more.
